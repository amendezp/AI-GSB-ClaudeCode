● Your Cheat Sheet

What is Claude Code?

A highly agentic coding assistant by Anthropic. It reads your files, writes code, runs commands, and builds entire projects. Fundamentally a CLI, but today we use it through the Desktop app.

Why Is Everyone Talking About It?

Claude Opus 4 (June 2025) and Opus 4.5 (Feb 2026) changed everything: production-quality code, true agentic behavior, larger context windows, and deep tool integration via MCP.

Permission Modes

Ask Permissions
Approves each action. Best for learning.
Plan Mode
Plans first, then executes. Great for complex tasks.
Auto Accept
Full autonomy. Fast, but use with caution.

Models

Opus 4.6 — Most capable
Sonnet 4.6 — Fast + capable Haiku 4.5 — Fastest

How to Use It

  • Open the Claude Desktop app
  • Select a project folder
  • Describe what you want in plain English
  • Review and approve changes
  • Iterate together

Key Concepts

CLAUDE.md — Memory file that persists across sessions

MCP — Protocol letting Claude use external tools

Worktree — Sandbox to experiment safely

Take These With You

3 Lessons to Walk Away With

Keep these in mind long after today

Lesson 1

Think Before You Build

Do not outsource your thinking. AI can build fast, but speed without direction is just expensive chaos.

Before every prototype, ask yourself three questions: What am I building? Why does it matter? And who is it for? If you can't answer clearly, Claude can't help you either.
Lesson 2

A Prototype Is NOT a Product

You can't vibe code your way to product-market fit. Prototypes are meant to test and validate ideas.

Real products require scale, reliability, and very high levels of security — especially AI applications. What we build today is a starting point for learning, not a launchpad to production.
Lesson 3

Agency & Accountability

The most important lesson: don't forget what makes us human.

You are responsible for what your agents build. Every time you accept a recommendation or send a prompt, you are taking an action for which you are accountable. AI is a powerful tool — but the judgment, ethics, and decisions remain yours.
